Brevard homeowner guide

Do you need a permit to remove a stump in Brevard County?

Most basic stump grinding after a tree has already been removed is a yard-service question, not a major permit project. But homeowners should still think about utilities, HOA rules, protected trees, sidewalks, easements, and whether the work is tied to larger construction.

This page is not legal advice and does not replace city, county, utility, or HOA rules. It gives homeowners a practical checklist before requesting a stump quote.

Usually straightforward

  • Grinding a leftover stump in a private yard after tree removal.
  • Lowering a stump below grade so the area can be mowed, mulched, or sodded.
  • Cleaning up a palm, pine, oak, or ornamental stump away from utilities and hardscape.

Check first when...

  • The stump is near sidewalks, road right-of-way, utility boxes, gas/electric/water lines, or drainage structures.
  • The property has HOA or community landscaping rules.
  • The stump is part of a larger tree removal, construction, driveway, fence, pool, or hardscape project.
  • You need roots removed, not just the visible stump ground below grade.

Quote prep checklist

  1. Know the city: Melbourne, Palm Bay, West Melbourne, Viera, Rockledge, Cocoa, Merritt Island, Titusville, or another Brevard area.
  2. Photograph the stump and nearby objects: fences, sprinklers, utility boxes, sidewalks, patios, pool screens, and landscape beds.
  3. Mark visible irrigation heads, lighting, or shallow utility concerns before work.
  4. If the stump is tied to a permitted building/fence/patio project, confirm requirements with the project contractor or local office first.
