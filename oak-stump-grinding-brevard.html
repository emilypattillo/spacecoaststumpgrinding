The heavyweight division

What makes oak its own job

1

Density & diameter

Live oak grinds slow, and Brevard's old yard oaks run 30, 40, 50+ inches across. Time-on-stump drives price, and oak buys more time per inch than anything else local yards grow.

2

Surface root sprawl

Oaks send lateral roots proud of the lawn for yards in every direction. The grind covers the flare and nearby visible roots; reclaiming the wider lawn is scoped by what you want back.

3

Chip volume

A landmark oak produces a genuinely surprising chip pile. Backfill takes what the hole wants; the surplus gets raked to beds or hauled — quoted as a visible line, decided by you.

Worth knowing

Sprouts, settling, and the long tail of a big oak

Two honest afterwords on big hardwoods. Oaks can sucker from surviving roots for a season — mowing the sprouts or spot-treating them ends it, and it's normal, not a botched grind. And a big oak's chip-backfilled crater settles for a couple of years as the chips and remaining roots decompose: top it off with soil before sod, and expect to add a little more later. Both go in the handoff conversation, because surprises age worse than stumps do.

Oak questions

Oak stump questions

Why do oak stumps cost more than the same-width pine or palm?

Density — live oak is among the hardest wood a grinder meets, and grinding time scales with how hard the teeth have to work. Same diameter, more passes, slower feed. The ballpark accounts for it the moment you say “oak,” which is why naming the tree helps the phone number land close.

Our oak's surface roots run all over the lawn. Do those grind too?

The visible ones near the stump, yes — chasing them is extra passes, scoped by how far you want the lawn reclaimed. Roots that have traveled under driveways or foundations are a different conversation than lawn roots, and you'll get a straight read on where grinding stops making sense.

Did we need a permit to remove the oak in the first place?

Possibly — several Brevard jurisdictions protect larger and specimen trees, and removal permits attach to the living tree. Grinding the stump of a tree that's already down is generally the uncomplicated part. The permit page covers the landscape honestly, including the it-varies-by-city truth.
