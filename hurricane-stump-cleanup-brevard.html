Storm and hurricane cleanup

Hurricane stump cleanup in Brevard County

After a hurricane or tropical storm, tree cleanup often happens in stages. Branches are cleared first, trunks are cut and hauled, and the stump is left for later. This guide explains what to send when requesting stump grinding after storm cleanup.

Storm-related details that matter

  • Whether the tree has already been cut flush or is still partly attached.
  • Whether debris blocks equipment access.
  • Whether the stump is close to fences, pool screens, driveways, irrigation, or utility equipment.
  • Whether there are several stumps from the same cleanup.
  • Whether the yard is wet, soft, rutted, or hard to access after rain.

Good photos to send

  1. A wide photo from the driveway or access path.
  2. A close photo of the stump and root flare.
  3. A photo showing nearby fences, utilities, sprinklers, or pool screens.
  4. Any photos showing remaining debris that may affect access.

Planning ahead

If you are coordinating tree removal, fence repair, landscaping, or sod after a storm, ask about the stump early. Leaving it until the end can slow down yard repair, new sod, mulch, or hardscape work.
